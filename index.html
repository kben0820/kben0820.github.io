Michael Joseph Jackson

Born: August 29, 1958 / Died: June 25, 2009



Bio:

Known as the "King of Pop", Michael Jackson is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century. He was an American singer, songwriter, dancer and philanthropist. During his four-decade career, his contributions to music, dance, and fashion, made him a global figure in popular culture. He influenced artists across many music genres. Though stage and video performances, he popularized complicated street dance moves such as the moonwalk, which he named, as well as the robot.
